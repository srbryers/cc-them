# Sources: Harvey Smith

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, or posts. No paywalled sources.

## Talks & Conferences

- [What Happened Here? Environmental Storytelling](https://gdcvault.com/play/1012647/What-Happened-Here-Environmental) — GDC Vault, 2010. Presented with Matthias Worch. Foundational talk on communicating narrative through props, scripted events, texturing, lighting, and scene composition. Defines the environmental storytelling methodology. Full slides available at [worch.com](https://www.worch.com/files/gdc/What_Happened_Here_Web_Notes.pdf).

- [Empowering the Player in a Story-Rich World: Co-Directing Dishonored](https://gdcvault.com/play/1018062/Empowering-the-Player-in-a) — GDC Vault, 2013. Presented with Raphael Colantonio. Covers pull-based narrative, player improvisation, systemic world design, and how Dishonored was co-directed to balance story richness with player freedom. Also archived at [Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/GDC2013Smith_201511).

- [What Happened Here? — witchboy.net mirror](https://www.witchboy.net/articles/what-happened-here/) — Harvey Smith's personal archive of the GDC 2010 talk notes. Confirms the framing of environmental storytelling as a pull vs. push model for narrative information.

## Essays & Articles

- [Player Character Concepts](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/player-character-concepts) — Game Developer, November 1999. Core argument that a strongly defined player-character identity is at odds with the player's own identity; case for the "lightweight, one-liner story" protagonist. Archived on gamedeveloper.com.

- [Designing Enemies With Distinct Functions](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/designing-enemies-with-distinct-functions) — Game Developer, April 1999. Establishes functional differentiation as a design principle: enemies should force the player to think differently, not just calculate harder. Includes the crocodile example.

- [The Future of Game Design: Moving Beyond Deus Ex and Other Dated Paradigms](https://www.witchboy.net/articles/the-future-of-game-design-moving-beyond-deus-ex-and-other-dated-paradigms/) — witchboy.net. Smith's self-critical assessment of Deus Ex's limitations and the directions he believed game design needed to move. Key source on his view of simulation, possibility space, and the constraints of the genre he helped define.

- [Systemic Level Design](https://www.witchboy.net/articles/systemic-level-design/) — witchboy.net. Defines systemic level design as gameplay arising from combinations of elements with globally consistent behaviors. Covers emergent play, reduced learning curves, and the contrast with scripted sequences.

- [The Dungeon Master: An Interview with Gary Gygax](https://www.witchboy.net/articles/the-dungeon-master-an-interview-with-gary-gygax/) — witchboy.net (originally Game Developer, 2002). Smith interviews Gygax on RPG design, player agency, and the transition from tabletop to digital. Reveals Smith's intellectual lineage and his view of player authorship as rooted in the tabletop RPG tradition. Also at [gamedeveloper.com](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/business/the-dungeon-master-an-interview-with-gary-gygax).

## Interviews

- [Dishonored's Harvey Smith: Player choice is paramount in design](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/-i-dishonored-i-s-harvey-smith-player-choice-is-paramount-in-design) — Game Developer (Gamasutra), 2012. Pre-launch interview. Contains his direct statement about rejecting linear corridor design and committing to multiple solutions, stylistic approaches, and moral compasses.

- [Harvey Smith on growing as a developer from Deus Ex to Dishonored](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/harvey-smith-on-growing-as-a-developer-from-i-deus-ex-i-to-i-dishonored-i-) — Game Developer, 2012. Covers how Smith's design philosophy developed from Ion Storm to Arkane, the influence of Looking Glass Studios, and how Dishonored was conceived as a return to smart systemic design.

- [Harvey Smith on environmental storytelling and embedding narrative](https://niemanstoryboard.org/2011/01/14/harvey-smith-on-environmental-storytelling-and-embedding-narrative/) — Nieman Storyboard (Harvard), January 2011. Interview specifically on narrative embedding and environmental storytelling. Contains his statement that it must be possible to miss some things to make finding them meaningful.

- [Harvey Smith, game director (Deus Ex, Dishonored, Redfall)](https://shows.acast.com/my-perfect-console/episodes/harvey-smith-game-director-deus-ex-dishonored-redfall) — My Perfect Console with Simon Parkin, Acast, December 2025. Wide-ranging biographical conversation covering his Air Force background, System Shock origins, Ion Storm, Arkane, and his design values across a full career.
