# Sources: BJ Fogg

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, or websites.

## Talks & Conferences

- [Forget big change, start with a tiny habit | BJ Fogg at TEDxFremont](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=AdKUJxjn-R8) — TEDxFremont. The foundational short talk where Fogg introduces the Tiny Habits method: anchor to an existing routine, shrink the behavior, celebrate immediately. Transcript available at singjupost.com.

- [Tiny Surprises for Happiness and Health | BJ Fogg, PhD | TEDxMaui](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=2L1R7OtJhWs) — TEDxMaui. Fogg introduces "The Maui Habit" — saying "It's going to be a great day" upon waking — as a demonstration of how celebration wires positive emotion into the first moments of each day.

## Official Websites & Lab Resources

- [Fogg Behavior Model — behaviormodel.org](https://www.behaviormodel.org/) — Fogg's primary reference site for the B=MAP model. Covers motivation, ability, prompt, the action line, and the three prompt types (spark, facilitator, signal). Freely accessible.

- [B=MAP Reference: Ability Factors — behaviormodel.org](https://www.behaviormodel.org/ability) — Detailed breakdown of the six ability factors (time, money, physical effort, mental effort, social deviance, non-routine nature) that determine whether a behavior is above or below the action line.

- [Stanford Behavior Design Lab — behaviordesign.stanford.edu](https://behaviordesign.stanford.edu/) — The official Stanford lab page. Covers the lab's research mission, Fogg's academic role (Adjunct Professor in the School of Medicine), and the shift from Persuasive Technology Lab to Behavior Design Lab.

- [Fogg Behavior Model | Behavior Design Lab — Stanford](https://behaviordesign.stanford.edu/resources/fogg-behavior-model) — Stanford's own resource page for the model, confirming the academic framing of B=MAP.

- [BJ Fogg's personal site — bjfogg.com](https://www.bjfogg.com/) — Overview of his books, speaking work, and the Tiny Habits method. Includes links to the free five-day Tiny Habits program.

- [Tiny Habits — tinyhabits.com](https://tinyhabits.com/) — The home of the Tiny Habits program. Documents the recipe format (After I [ANCHOR], I will [TINY BEHAVIOR], then I will [CELEBRATE]) and the method's grounding in the B=MAP model.

## Books (Publicly Referenced)

- [Tiny Habits: The Small Changes That Change Everything — Goodreads](https://www.goodreads.com/book/show/43261127-tiny-habits) — BJ Fogg, 2019. New York Times bestseller. The primary source for the Tiny Habits method, anchor-based habit design, celebration mechanics, and the critique of willpower-based models.

- [Persuasive Technology: Using Computers to Change What We Think and Do — Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/persuasivetechno0000fogg) — BJ Fogg, 2003. The founding text of captology. Introduces the functional triad (computers as tool, media, and social actor) and the academic framework for technology-driven behavior change. Freely available via Internet Archive.

## Podcasts & Interviews

- [306: BJ Fogg | Tiny Habits That Change Everything — The Jordan Harbinger Show](https://www.jordanharbinger.com/bj-fogg-tiny-habits-that-change-everything/) — January 2020. Wide-ranging conversation covering the B=MAP model, why motivation fails as a primary strategy, the Tiny Habits recipe, and the role of celebration in wiring habits.

- [Create Lasting Change | BJ Fogg on The Knowledge Project with Shane Parrish — Podcast Notes](https://podcastnotes.org/knowledge-project/bj-fogg/) — The Knowledge Project episode covering behavior change, the action line, motivation waves, and practical habit design. Summary and notes freely available.
