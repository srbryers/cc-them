# Sources: Vanessa Van Edwards

All sources are freely available public recordings, articles, or posts.

## Books

- [Captivate: The Science of Succeeding with People](https://www.scienceofpeople.com/captivate/) — Portfolio/Penguin, 2017. The foundational text: first-five-minutes mechanics, conversation sparkers, hot buttons, the Triple Threat, personality speed-reading via the Big Five, and the social game plan framework. Translated into 17 languages.
- [Cues: Master the Secret Language of Charismatic Communication](https://www.scienceofpeople.com/cues/) — Portfolio/Penguin, 2022. Wall Street Journal bestseller. Systematizes the warmth-competence matrix, names the four cue channels (nonverbal, vocal, verbal, imagery), and provides the Charisma Zone framework. Core source for the cue taxonomy used throughout the agent.

## Talks

- [You Are Contagious | TEDxLondon](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cef35Fk7YD8) — TEDxLondon, 2017. Van Edwards's viral talk (5M+ views) on emotional contagion, the nonverbal behaviors that separate high-performing TED speakers from low-performing ones, and the practical takeaway that cue production changes the emotional state of every person in the room.

## Articles (scienceofpeople.com)

- [Cues: Master the Secret Language of Charismatic Communication — Overview](https://www.scienceofpeople.com/cues/) — Science of People. Covers the four cue channels, warmth vs. competence definitions, the Charisma Zone, and the Princeton research finding that warmth and competence account for 82% of social impressions.
- [Captivate Summary & Review](https://www.scienceofpeople.com/captivate-summary/) — Science of People. Comprehensive summary of the Captivate framework: first-five-minutes hacks, conversation sparkers, the Triple Threat, hot buttons, and the social game plan.
- [Secrets of a Successful TED Talk](https://www.scienceofpeople.com/secrets-of-a-successful-ted-talk/) — Science of People. Documents the 760-volunteer study in which participants rated hundreds of TED Talks for nonverbal cues. Key findings: hand gestures correlate with views; opinions form in the first few seconds; body language ratings on mute matched ratings with sound.
- [Cues Book Summary & Review](https://www.scienceofpeople.com/cues-book-summary/) — Science of People. Extended summary of the Cues book with additional commentary on the warmth-competence split, the four channels, and technique examples.

## Podcast Interviews

- [Vanessa Van Edwards | How to Captivate with Social Cues — The Jordan Harbinger Show, Episode 30](https://www.jordanharbinger.com/vanessa-van-edwards-how-to-captivate-with-social-cues/) — Jordan Harbinger Show. Early comprehensive interview covering the Science of People research program, the Captivate framework, first-impression cues, conversation sparkers, and personality reading. Freely available.
- [Vanessa Van Edwards | The Science of Succeeding with People — The Jordan Harbinger Show, Episode 670](https://www.jordanharbinger.com/vanessa-van-edwards-the-science-of-succeeding-with-people/) — Jordan Harbinger Show, 2022. Follow-up interview covering the Cues framework, nonverbal and vocal cue channels, how photographs and dating profiles signal warmth vs. competence, and patterns from Shark Tank analysis.
- [Vanessa Van Edwards | Pumping up the Volume of Nonverbal Communication — The Jordan Harbinger Show](https://www.jordanharbinger.com/vanessa-van-edwards-pumping-up-the-volume-of-nonverbal-communication/) — Jordan Harbinger Show. Deep dive on vocal cues: pace, tone, volume, and how vocal delivery can undercut or amplify verbal content. Covers the gap between what people intend to communicate and what their cues actually signal.
