# Sources: Don Norman

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, interviews, or posts. No paywalled content.

## Talks & Conferences

- [3 Ways Good Design Makes You Happy](https://www.ted.com/talks/don_norman_3_ways_good_design_makes_you_happy) — TED2003. Norman's foundational public talk on the three levels of emotional design (visceral, behavioral, reflective) and why attractive things work better. Widely cited as the accessible introduction to *Emotional Design*.

- [It's Not You. Bad Doors Are Everywhere.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yY96hTb8WgI) — Vox / 99% Invisible, 2016. Short documentary featuring Norman on "Norman Doors" — objects whose design signals the wrong action. Demonstrates affordances, signifiers, and the gulf of execution through everyday examples. Over 10 million views.

- [Design for the 21st Century](https://www.interaction-design.org/courses/design-for-the-21st-century) — Interaction Design Foundation master class. Norman's course on the evolving scope of design — from individual usability to systemic, humanity-centered approaches. Covers complexity vs. complication, societal scale, and designer responsibility.

## Essays & Blog Posts

- [Humanity-Centered versus Human-Centered Design](https://jnd.org/humanity-centered-versus-human-centered-design/) — jnd.org, March 2023. Norman's clearest statement of why he moved beyond human-centered design: it optimizes for the individual while ignoring environmental and social systems. Defines "humanity-centered" as accounting for all people and the ecosystem.

- [Examples and Opportunities in Humanity-Centered Design](https://jnd.org/humanity-centered-design/) — jnd.org, March 2023. Companion essay with concrete applications of humanity-centered framing — circular economy, community co-design, addressing systemic inequality through design practice.

- [Emotional Design: People and Things](https://jnd.org/emotional-design-people-and-things/) — jnd.org. Essay elaborating the visceral/behavioral/reflective model and how emotional responses at each level interact and sometimes conflict.

- [Essay & Articles Index](https://jnd.org/essay-articles/) — jnd.org. Full archive of Norman's public essays spanning affordances, signifiers, emotional design, aging, education, and humanity-centered design. Freely accessible.

## Interviews & Podcasts

- [Author Talks: Don Norman Designs a Better World](https://www.mckinsey.com/featured-insights/mckinsey-on-books/author-talks-don-norman-designs-a-better-world) — McKinsey & Company, April 2023. Video interview on *Design for a Better World* (MIT Press, 2023). Covers circular economy, AI, humanity-centered design, and the limits of optimizing for individual users. A video and text summary is also available on [jnd.org](https://jnd.org/a-video-and-text-summary-of-design-for-a-better-world-a-talk-for-mckinsey-company/).

- [Embracing Humanity-Centered Design with Don Norman](https://www.usertesting.com/resources/podcast/what-is-humanity-centered-design) — Insights Unlocked (UserTesting podcast), May 2024. 37-minute conversation covering the evolution from human-centered to humanity-centered design, AI's role in design, and Norman's critique of products designed for short lifespans. Also on [Apple Podcasts](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/embracing-humanity-centered-design-with-don-norman/id1624560671?i=1000654645752).

- [Design Matters: Don Norman](https://www.printmag.com/podcasts/2023/design-matters-don-norman/) — PRINT Magazine / Design Matters with Debbie Millman, March 2023. Wide-ranging conversation on *Design for a Better World*, human behavior, and Norman's view of design's responsibility in addressing systemic crises.

- [Norman Doors: Don't Know Whether to Push or Pull? Blame Design.](https://99percentinvisible.org/article/norman-doors-dont-know-whether-push-pull-blame-design/) — 99% Invisible article and episode, Roman Mars. Written companion to the Norman Door video. Articulates the core principle: when a design fails, blame the design, not the user.

## Books (referenced for philosophy, available publicly)

- *The Design of Everyday Things* (revised and expanded edition, 2013) — MIT Press / Basic Books. The source for affordances, signifiers, gulfs of execution and evaluation, the seven stages of action, slips vs. mistakes, and the principle of blaming design rather than users. Book page at [nngroup.com](https://www.nngroup.com/books/design-everyday-things-revised/).

- *Emotional Design: Why We Love (or Hate) Everyday Things* (2004) — Basic Books. Source for the visceral/behavioral/reflective framework. Book page at [nngroup.com](https://www.nngroup.com/books/emotional-design/).

- *Design for a Better World: Meaningful, Sustainable, Humanity Centered* (2023) — MIT Press. Source for humanity-centered design, circular economy framing, and Norman's critique of human-centered design's limited scope. Book page at [jnd.org](https://jnd.org/books/design-for-a-better-world/).
