# Sources: Joel Burgess

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, slides, or posts.

## GDC Talks & Transcripts

- [Skyrim's Modular Level Design — GDC 2013 Transcript](http://blog.joelburgess.com/2013/04/skyrims-modular-level-design-gdc-2013.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Full transcript of the Level Design in a Day talk co-presented with Nate Purkeypile. Covers the kit philosophy, the Mondrian analogy, compositional variation, and art fatigue.

- [Skyrim's Modular Approach to Level Design](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/skyrim-s-modular-approach-to-level-design) — Game Developer (Gamasutra). Written companion piece to the GDC 2013 talk, covering the modular pipeline and its roots in Bethesda's 18-year kit-building history.

- [GDC 2013 Slides: Skyrim's Modular Level Design](https://www.slideshare.net/JoelBurgess/3-10gdc2014-iterativeleveldesignprocess) — SlideShare. Presentation deck from the Level Design in a Day bootcamp.

- [GDC 2014 Transcript: The Iterative Level Design Process Used to Ship Fallout 3 and Skyrim](http://blog.joelburgess.com/2014/07/gdc-2014-transcript-iterative-level.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Full transcript of the 2014 Level Design in a Day talk. Covers the whitebox-first pipeline, playtest loops, and how Fallout 3 established Bethesda's dedicated level design team.

- [GDC 2014: Iterative Level Design — Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/GDC2014Burgess2) — archive.org. Free audio/video download of the full GDC 2014 talk.

- [GDC Vault: Level Design in a Day — Iterative Level Design to Ship Skyrim and Fallout 3](https://www.gdcvault.com/play/1020777/Level-Design-in-a-Day) — GDC Vault. Official GDC listing for the 2014 talk (free access tier available).

- [GDC 2016: Fallout 4's Modular Level Design — Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/GDC2016Burgess) — archive.org. Free download of the full GDC 2016 talk co-presented with Nate Purkeypile, extending the modular kit philosophy to Fallout 4.

- [GDC Vault: Fallout 4's Modular Level Design](https://gdcvault.com/play/1023202/-Fallout-4-s-Modular) — GDC Vault. Official listing for the 2016 talk.

- [GDC 2011 Transcript: Motivating Players in Open World Games](http://blog.joelburgess.com/2011/03/gdc-2011-transcript-motivating-players.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Full transcript of the Level Design in a Day 2011 talk. Covers breadcrumbing, how players prioritize goals, and the mechanics of curiosity in open worlds.

- [GDC 2011 Slides: Motivating Players in Open Worlds](https://www.slideshare.net/JoelBurgess/gdc2011-motivating-players-in-open-worlds) — SlideShare. Presentation deck for the 2011 talk.

- [GDC 2010 Slides: Level Designer, Storyteller](http://blog.joelburgess.com/2010/03/gdc-slides-level-designer-storyteller.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Slides from the inaugural Level Design in a Day bootcamp talk. Covers environmental storytelling and snapshot stories in Fallout 3.

## Blog Posts & Dev Diaries

- [Joel Burgess Blog — Appearances, Talks & Articles](http://blog.joelburgess.com/p/design-talks.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Index of all public talks, articles, and appearances. Primary reference for confirmed public sources.

- [Fallout 3 Dev Diary: Level Design](http://blog.joelburgess.com/p/fallout-3-dev-diary-level-design_28.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Dev diary covering the design of Fallout 3's world — blurring the lines between town, wilderness, and dungeon; connectivity; and the challenge of believable post-apocalyptic Washington D.C.

- [Fallout 3 Dev Diary: Point Lookout](http://blog.joelburgess.com/p/fallout-3-dev-diary-level-design.html) — blog.joelburgess.com. Co-written with Nate Purkeypile. Covers the world design approach for the Point Lookout DLC.
