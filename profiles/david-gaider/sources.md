# Sources: David Gaider

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, posts, or articles.

## Talks & Panels

- [BioWare at GaymerX2: Building a Better Romance](https://blog.bioware.com/2014/07/10/bioware-at-gaymerx2/) — BioWare Blog, 2014. Announcement and links to the panel audio. David Gaider, Patrick Weekes, Karin Weekes, and Jessica Merizan discuss romance design, sexual orientation in companion characters, and representation philosophy for Dragon Age: Inquisition.

- [NYU Game Center Lecture: Dragon Age: Inquisition Storytelling](https://gamecenter.nyu.edu/event/dragon-age-inquisition/) — NYU Game Center, December 2014. Panel with creative director Mike Laidlaw, lead writer David Gaider, and actor Freddie Prinze Jr. on BioWare's approach to storytelling, companion design, and world-building in Inquisition. Covered by Kotaku and Technical.ly.

- [Stray Gods — How Summerfall is Turning an RPG into a Musical](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/how-stray-gods-is-turning-a-musical-into-an-rpg) — Game Developer, 2023. Gaider discusses the trait-based dialogue system (Kickass / Clever / Charming) and how personality-driven branching differs from moral-alignment branching.

## Long-form Interviews

- [Romance in Dragon Age](https://gameinformer.com/b/features/archive/2013/08/28/romance-in-dragon-age.aspx) — Game Informer, August 2013. Gaider's most comprehensive public statement on romance design philosophy: approval as gate vs. flavor, pacing, Leliana vs. Morrigan as contrasting romantic archetypes, opt-in flirting mechanics, and the limits of making all companions romanceable.

- [David Gaider on Intimacy in Baldur's Gate II and BioWare RPGs](https://www.shacknews.com/article/103473/beneath-a-starless-sky-pillars-of-eternity-and-the-infinity-engine-era-of-rpgs?page=15) — Shacknews, 2017. Gaider discusses the accidental origins of romance in Baldur's Gate II, his first romance writing work (Anomen), and why the team was surprised by the positive player response. Mirrored on Beamdog Forums.

- [David Gaider Interview: How Dragon Age 2's Raw, Violent Story Was Written In One Draft](https://www.thegamer.com/dragon-age-2-david-gaider-interview/) — The Gamer, 2021. Retrospective on DA2's writing process, the friendship/rivalry system design intent, the communication failure around rivalry as a valid path, and what Gaider wishes had been done differently.

- [David Gaider on the Creation of Dragon Age: Inquisition's Dorian](https://www.thegamer.com/dragon-age-inquisition-dorian-david-gaider-interview/) — The Gamer, 2021. Gaider discusses writing Dorian as a gay character whose sexuality was central to his arc, the distinction between identity-as-story vs. identity-as-incidental, and the volume of personal mail he received from players about Dorian.

- [The Man Who Built Thedas: Interview with David Gaider](https://mcvuk.com/development-news/the-man-who-built-thedas-interview-with-david-gaider/) — MCV/Develop, 2016. Interview from Gaider's time at Beamdog covering world-building philosophy, the importance of characters as entry points into setting, and how Thedas was constructed region by region.

## Essays & Gamedeveloper Articles

- [The Story Thing: BioWare's David Gaider Speaks](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/the-story-thing-bioware-s-david-gaider-speaks) — Game Developer (Gamasutra). Gaider on the evolution of BioWare's storytelling from Baldur's Gate II through Dragon Age: Origins — how followers became the primary vehicle for player investment in the world, and the role of party banter in character development.

- [The Writing of BioWare's Dragon Age II: David Gaider Speaks](https://www.gamedeveloper.com/design/the-writing-of-bioware-s-i-dragon-age-ii-i-david-gaider-speaks) — Game Developer (Gamasutra). Post-release analysis of DA2's writing approach, the friendship/rivalry system mechanics, and the constraints the game's compressed development schedule placed on companion arcs.

- ["I Want to Write Video Games"](https://medium.com/@davidgaider/i-want-to-write-video-games-d83da40fdf8e) — Medium, David Gaider. Essay on the realities of game writing as a career. Introduces the framing that "a full 75% of a game writer's job is hacking and suturing their own story to solve problems which have nothing to do with narrative quality," and that game writing is creative management, not auteur expression.

## Forewords & Community Posts

- [Fortitudo Foreword by David Gaider](https://dorianartbook.tumblr.com/post/170496044525/foreword-by-david-gaider-i-wrote-dorian-over-a) — Tumblr / Fortitudo artbook, 2018. Gaider's personal account of writing Dorian, including the moment Dorian's sexuality "clicked" his arc into place, his own experience informing the character, and the personal correspondence from players the character generated.

- [BioWare Lead Writer Defends Dragon Age II Romance Options](https://www.gameinformer.com/b/news/archive/2011/03/24/bioware-lead-writer-defends-dragon-age-ii-romance-options.aspx) — Game Informer, March 2011. Gaider's public response to player backlash over DA2's bisexual romance options — an early, direct public statement on representation and the "straight male gamer" letter, widely cited as a landmark moment in games industry discourse on inclusive design.

- [Dragon Age Development Insights from David Gaider](https://felassan.tumblr.com/post/630420622367997952/dragon-age-development-insights-from-david-gaider) — felassan.tumblr.com. Aggregated compilation of Gaider's public social media posts and interviews on Dragon Age design decisions, companion writing, and the approval/rivalry system. Useful secondary source for tracing statements back to primary contexts.

## Video Interviews

- [David Gaider: Storytelling, Dragon Age and BioWare — Gamelab 2015 Interview](https://www.gamereactor.eu/david-gaider-storytelling-dragon-age-and-bioware/) — Gamereactor. Video interview from Gamelab 2015 in Barcelona. Gaider discusses the writers' room process for Inquisition's eleven or twelve major companion arcs, the collaborative structure of companion design, and the team's approach to side content and player feedback.

- [How Stray Gods Embraces Its Inner Theatre Geek](https://www.thegamer.com/stray-gods-interview-david-gaider-austin-wintory/) — The Gamer, 2023. Interview with Gaider and composer Austin Wintory on Stray Gods' trait system, the relationship between player personality and musical outcomes, and how narrative branching without moral-alignment framing changes player investment.
