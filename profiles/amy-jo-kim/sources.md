# Sources: Amy Jo Kim

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, blog posts, or book listings.

## Books

- [Game Thinking: Innovate smarter & drive deep engagement with design techniques from hit games](https://www.amazon.com/Game-Thinking-Innovate-engagement-techniques/dp/099978854X) — Amy Jo Kim, Scott Kim (foreword by Raph Koster), 2018. The primary text for the Game Thinking framework: superfans, core learning loop, player journey, storyboarding as validation.
- [Community Building on the Web: Secret Strategies for Successful Online Communities](https://www.oreilly.com/library/view/community-building-on/0201874849/) — Amy Jo Kim, Peachpit, 2000. Foundational text on social architecture for online communities; covers roles, rituals, events, and the dynamics of community growth over time.

## Blog Posts (amyjokim.com)

- [The Player's Journey: Designing Over Time](https://amyjokim.com/blog/2012/09/14/the-players-journey-designing-over-time/) — amyjokim.com, 2012. Introduces the Newbie → Regular → Enthusiast model and the principle that great designs are compelling because the player's experience changes over time.
- [Core Learning Loops in Game Design & Enterprise Change Management](https://amyjokim.com/blog/2016/08/25/core-learning-loops-game-design-enterprise-change-management/) — amyjokim.com, 2016. Covers the structure of the core learning loop, Raph Koster's influence, and how the loop pattern applies beyond games.
- [Super-charge Your Core Loop with Feedback & Progress](https://amyjokim.com/blog/2014/05/29/super-charge-your-core-loop-with-feedback-and-progress/) — amyjokim.com, 2014. Explains why feedback quality is the differentiating factor in pleasurable learning engines; introduces the flow-state connection.
- [Entice Me Back: How Core Loops Drive Re-Engagement](https://amyjokim.com/blog/2014/05/27/entice-me-back-how-core-loops-drive-re-engagement/) — amyjokim.com, 2014. On habit-forming systems and the role of the core loop in re-engagement across games and consumer products.
- [Beyond Player Types: Kim's Social Action Matrix](https://amyjokim.com/blog/2014/02/28/beyond-player-types-kims-social-action-matrix/) — amyjokim.com, 2014. Introduces the Social Action Matrix (Compete, Explore, Express, Collaborate) as a replacement for fixed player-type personality models.
- [7 Gamification Myths, Debunked](https://amyjokim.com/blog/2014/10/04/7-gamification-myths-debunked/) — amyjokim.com, 2014. Direct critique of surface-level gamification; distinguishes game mechanics as visible artifacts from the underlying experience they are meant to support.

## Medium Articles

- [Game Thinking, Explained](https://amyjokim.medium.com/game-thinking-explained-fa6da3e8debb) — Medium, Amy Jo Kim. Overview of the three pillars of Game Thinking: superfans, core learning loop, and path to mastery.
- [Introduction to Game Thinking](https://medium.com/@amyjokim/introduction-to-game-thinking-6e7273e68cc9) — Medium, Amy Jo Kim. Explains how the most powerful product story is the personal narrative of customer transformation.

## Podcasts & Interviews

- [Building Successful Products With Game Thinking — Tech Lead Journal, Episode 127](https://techleadjournal.dev/episodes/127/) — Tech Lead Journal. Kim walks through the three core ideas: identifying superfans, building a sticky core habit loop, and validating product concepts with storyboards.
- [Game Thinking for Product Innovation — Barry O'Reilly's Unlearn Podcast](https://barryoreilly.com/explore/podcast/game-thinking-for-product-innovation-with-amy-jo-kim/) — Barry O'Reilly. Covers Kim's work on Rock Band and The Sims; her argument for designing the customer journey rather than individual features; intrinsic vs. extrinsic motivation.
- [Level Up Your Community with Amy Jo Kim's Principles of Game Thinking — Community Signal](https://www.communitysignal.com/level-up-your-community-with-amy-jo-kims-principles-of-game-thinking/) — Community Signal podcast. Applies the Game Thinking framework specifically to community building; covers journey design, retention, and evolving mechanics as communities mature.

## Presentations (SlideShare)

- [Smart Gamification](https://www.slideshare.net/amyjokim/smart-gamification) — SlideShare, Amy Jo Kim. Slide deck contrasting smart gamification (progression mechanics that support mastery) with dumb gamification (mechanics applied without a compelling core experience). Includes the trinity of intrinsic motivation: autonomy, mastery, purpose.
- [Gamification 101: Design the Player Journey](https://www.slideshare.net/amyjokim/gamification-101-design-the-player-journey) — SlideShare, Amy Jo Kim. Introduces the player journey framework for non-game product designers; covers onboarding, habit-building, and mastery stages.
