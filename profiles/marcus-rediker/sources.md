# Sources: Marcus Rediker

All sources are published academic works in print, freely-available academic lectures and interviews, or articles in open-access scholarly journals. Rediker is an active public scholar with extensive video lecture archives and many published interviews; sourcing for his stances is unusually well-documented for a working historian.

## Books (primary sources)

- *Between the Devil and the Deep Blue Sea: Merchant Seamen, Pirates and the Anglo-American Maritime World, 1700–1750* — Cambridge University Press, 1987. The foundational work; established the labor-history reading of the early-eighteenth-century merchant marine and laid the groundwork for the pirate-as-rebel-worker thesis.
- *Villains of All Nations: Atlantic Pirates in the Golden Age* — Beacon Press, 2004. The canonical academic history of Golden Age piracy (1716–1726); source of nearly every specific claim in this profile about pirate political form, articles, multi-racial composition, and shipboard life.
- *The Many-Headed Hydra: Sailors, Slaves, Commoners, and the Hidden History of the Revolutionary Atlantic* — Beacon Press, 2000 (with Peter Linebaugh). The expanded political framework of multi-racial Atlantic resistance; introduces "the hydrarchy" as the political form of the maritime working class.
- *The Slave Ship: A Human History* — Viking, 2007. Granular material history of the Atlantic slave trade as told from the deck of the ship; the methodological model for reading bureaucratic records "from below."
- *Outlaws of the Atlantic: Sailors, Pirates, and Motley Crews in the Age of Sail* — Beacon Press, 2014. Essay collection consolidating Rediker's framework across his major themes; useful as a single-volume introduction.
- *The Amistad Rebellion: An Atlantic Odyssey of Slavery and Freedom* — Viking, 2012. Case study of a specific slave-ship uprising; useful for understanding how Rediker reconstructs an event from fragmentary archival traces.
- *The Fearless Benjamin Lay: The Quaker Dwarf Who Became the First Revolutionary Abolitionist* — Beacon Press, 2017. Biography; demonstrates Rediker's method on land as well as at sea.

## Lectures & talks (free, public)

- **YouTube — "Marcus Rediker" lecture series.** Multiple full-length academic talks at universities (Pittsburgh, Berkeley, Yale) on piracy, the slave ship, and Atlantic working-class history. Search returns dozens of recorded talks, most 45–90 minutes.
- **The Atlantic Plantation Society lectures.** Rediker has given multiple keynote talks at academic conferences on Atlantic history that are publicly archived.
- **Bookstore + library promotional talks.** Around the publication of each book, Rediker did extensive on-camera interviews and bookstore Q&A sessions, many of which remain on YouTube.

## Interviews & podcasts

- **The Dig (Jacobin podcast)** — Episodes featuring Rediker on Golden Age piracy and the slave ship. Free podcast format; substantive multi-hour conversations.
- **History Extra (BBC History Magazine podcast)** — Rediker has been interviewed multiple times around the publication of his books.
- **NPR appearances** — Coverage around *The Slave Ship* (2007) and *Villains of All Nations* (2004); transcripts and audio archived on npr.org.

## Academic articles (selection)

- "The Pirate and the Gallows" — *Atlantic Studies*, several articles by Rediker on the public execution of pirates as imperial spectacle.
- "Hydrarchy and Libertalia" — multiple essays exploring the political form of pirate companies and their resonance with later revolutionary movements.
- Most of Rediker's journal articles are accessible through university library subscriptions; many also available on JSTOR Open Access or via authors' personal academic pages.

## Notes on sourcing limits

- Rediker's books are in print and copyright; quotations in the profile are paraphrased per cc-them convention.
- The agent's stances on specific period material details (clothing, food, weapons, ship layouts) are drawn from *Between the Devil and the Deep Blue Sea* and *Villains of All Nations*, which are the most-detailed material-culture sources in his corpus.
- His political framing (the hydrarchy, multi-racial Atlantic resistance) is collaborative with Peter Linebaugh in *The Many-Headed Hydra*; positions in those areas could equally be attributed to Linebaugh and should be cited as joint when precision matters.
- Rediker is a working academic with explicit political positions (broadly left, anti-imperial, labor-history-oriented); the profile honors this rather than scrubbing it. The agent should be a historian, not a neutral describer.
