# Sources: James Pennebaker

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, or posts. No paywalled sources.

## Talks & Lectures

- [The Secret Life of Pronouns — TEDxAustin](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=PGsQwAu3PzU) — TEDxAustin. Pennebaker's primary public talk on how function words (I, you, me, we) reveal psychological state, status, gender, and deception. The foundational 18-minute overview of his pronouns research for a general audience.

- [James Pennebaker — Expressive Writing and Health](https://liberalarts.utexas.edu/humanitiesinstitute/events/james-pennebaker-expressive-writing-and-health-how-putting-upheavals-into-words-can-affect-our-thoughts-feelings-and-behaviors) — University of Texas at Austin Humanities Institute. Public lecture on how putting emotional upheavals into words affects thoughts, feelings, and physical health. Covers the original 1983 experiments and subsequent replications.

- [LSA Public Lectures on Language — "Using Language Analysis to Read Minds"](https://www.linguisticsociety.org/content/lsa-public-lectures-language-series) — Linguistic Society of America, January 2017. Pennebaker was featured in the LSA's inaugural Public Lectures on Language series, speaking on computational approaches to psychological text analysis.

## Podcasts & Interviews

- [Expressive Writing Can Help Your Mental Health — Speaking of Psychology](https://www.apa.org/news/podcasts/speaking-of-psychology/expressive-writing) — American Psychological Association flagship podcast. Pennebaker explains how his research began, what the expressive writing protocol involves, how language analysis reveals psychological states, and the boundaries of what language can and cannot tell us.

- [Why Secrets Wreck Us: The Life-Changing Power of Expressive Writing — Good Life Project](https://www.goodlifeproject.com/podcast/the-life-changing-power-of-expressive-writing-james-w-pennebaker/) — Good Life Project, 2025. Wide-ranging interview covering expressive writing, emotional disclosure, the role of secrets in health, and Pennebaker's current thinking on the mechanism behind the writing effect.

- [Using Expressive Writing to Heal Trauma — The Weekend University](https://podcasts.apple.com/us/podcast/using-expressive-writing-to-heal-trauma-dr-james-pennebaker/id1233173966?i=1000504852343) — The Weekend University podcast. Recorded as part of the 2020 Holistic Change Summit. Covers the healing protocol in detail, common misconceptions, and how expressive writing differs from ordinary journaling.

- [The Secret Life of Pronouns — The Psych Files (Ep. 166)](http://thepsychfiles.com/2011/12/ep-166-the-secret-life-of-pronouns-an-interview-with-james-pennebaker/) — The Psych Files podcast, 2011. Interview focused on the book of the same name: function words as psychological signals, the I-word/narcissism misconception, Language Style Matching, and what small words reveal about status, gender, and honesty.

## Articles & Essays

- [Writing to Heal — APA Monitor on Psychology](https://www.apa.org/monitor/jun02/writing) — Monitor on Psychology, June 2002. Overview of Pennebaker's expressive writing research written for a professional psychology audience. Covers the health outcomes data, the protocol parameters, and competing theoretical explanations for the effect.

- [Language as a Vehicle to the Mind — Psi Chi Eye on Psi Chi](https://www.psichi.org/page/164EyeSum12kPennebak) — Psi Chi, Summer 2012. Accessible interview-style overview of Pennebaker's language analysis work: LIWC, function words, LSM, and what word patterns reveal about social relationships and psychological states.

- [The Secret Life of Pronouns: Computational Linguistics and What Our Word Choices Reveal About Us — The Marginalian](https://www.themarginalian.org/2011/09/01/the-secret-life-of-pronouns/) — The Marginalian (formerly Brain Pickings), September 2011. Synthesis of Pennebaker's book for a general audience, covering the core claims about function words, status, gender, and linguistic deception research.

## Institutional & Reference Pages

- [James W. Pennebaker — UT Austin Faculty Page](https://liberalarts.utexas.edu/psychology/faculty/pennebak) — University of Texas at Austin, Department of Psychology. Official faculty profile listing research areas, current projects, and selected publications.

- [The Secret Life of Pronouns — Author Website](https://www.secretlifeofpronouns.com/) — Pennebaker's official book site, including video resources demonstrating LIWC analysis and links to research summaries on pronoun psychology.

- [Expressive Writing in Psychological Science — Perspectives on Psychological Science (2018)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28992443/) — PubMed entry for Pennebaker's 2018 review article summarizing three decades of expressive writing research, meta-analytic effect sizes (d ≈ 0.16), protocol parameters, and open questions about mechanism. Full text available via the linked journal.
