# Sources: Emily Short

All sources are freely available public recordings, blog posts, or database entries.

## Blog — emshort.blog

- [Beyond Branching: Quality-Based, Salience-Based, and Waypoint Narrative Structures](https://emshort.blog/2016/04/12/beyond-branching-quality-based-and-salience-based-narrative-structures/) — emshort.blog, April 2016. The foundational essay defining quality-based narrative, salience-based narrative, and waypoint narrative as alternatives to traditional branching. Introduces the storylet vocabulary in its mature form and applies it to Fallen London, Firewatch, and Left 4 Dead.

- [Storylets: You Want Them](https://emshort.blog/2019/11/29/storylets-you-want-them/) — emshort.blog, November 2019. The definitive post on storylet architecture: content, prerequisites, and effects as the atomic narrative unit. Covers how storylets replicate classical branching structures (gauntlet, branch-and-bottleneck, sorting hat, loop-and-grow) and argues for their suitability for long-lived games with DLC.

- [Pacing Storylet Structures](https://emshort.blog/2020/01/21/pacing-storylet-structures/) — emshort.blog, January 2020. Develops visual diagramming methods for storylet pacing, introducing the principle that open sections feel less intense and constrained sections are appropriate for climactic moments. Covers rhombus (acceleration), wheel (randomized deck), and linear structures.

- [Mailbag: Development Process for Storylet-Based Interactive Fiction](https://emshort.blog/2020/02/18/mailbag-development-process-for-storylet-based-interactive-fiction/) — emshort.blog, February 2020. Practical process guide: planning narrative goals, designing quality systems thematically, building the authored spine first, and iterating around player guidance. Recommends Tiny QBN, ink, and Yarn Spinner.

- [Conversation as Gameplay Talk](https://emshort.blog/2019/01/20/conversation-as-gameplay-talk/) — emshort.blog, January 2019. Argues that dialogue must support intentional player action with learnable mechanics. Examines Diplomacy, Jenga, and combat as structural models. Covers dynamic text generation in the Character Engine where word choices shift based on emotional state and relationship.

- [Mailbag: Deep Conversation](https://emshort.blog/2018/09/11/mailbag-deep-conversation/) — emshort.blog, September 2018. Survey of conversation-centered game designs: exploration-focused dialogue (Subsurface Circular), factual reasoning (Phoenix Wright), temporal dynamics (Ladykiller in a Bind), and relationship modeling (Prom Week). Positions deep conversation as an architectural choice requiring upfront system design.

- [Mailbag: Knowledge-Driven Dialogue](https://emshort.blog/2019/06/11/mailbag-knowledge-driven-dialogue/) — emshort.blog, June 2019. On implementing NPC knowledge tracking in Inform 7. Distinguishes topic-unlocking approaches (Epistemology, Threaded Conversation, Glass) from sophisticated knowledge databases. References Spirit AI Character Engine as a more capable external system.

- [What Does Your Narrative System Need to Do?](https://emshort.blog/2022/04/09/what-does-your-narrative-system-need-to-do/) — emshort.blog, April 2022. Identifies four distinct functions narrative systems must handle: content selection/generation, player option management, action consequence resolution, and result communication. Argues that unlocking new options is often clearer and cheaper than branching content to show consequences.

- [Narrative States](https://emshort.blog/2019/11/23/narrative-states/) — emshort.blog, November 2019. Introduces ternary plots as a visual tool for mapping quality-based narrative outcome spaces. Demonstrates how designers can identify gaps, enlarge outcome zones, and add warning states before transitions.

- [Kreminski on Storylets](https://emshort.blog/2019/01/06/kreminski-on-storylets/) — emshort.blog, January 2019. Short's review of Max Kreminski's academic paper on storylet design dimensions: precondition types, repeatability, content types, and content selection architecture. Covers dynamic resource binding (the "sell [cargo] on [planet]" pattern) and the tool-accessibility gap.

- [Dialogue Expressiveness in Mask of the Rose](https://emshort.blog/2023/11/22/dialogue-expressiveness-in-mask-of-the-rose/) — emshort.blog, November 2023. Analysis of how Mask of the Rose surfaces a larger pool of dialogue options filtered by player personality traits and wardrobe, allowing players to form hypotheses about available options through a crafting system.

- [Describing a Procedurally Generated World](https://emshort.blog/2019/03/05/describing-a-procedurally-generated-world/) — emshort.blog, March 2019. On what makes procedural world description work: systematic identification of narratively important facts, layered text generation (generative grammars plus salience selection), and the knowledge-vs-reality distinction for authentic NPC responses.

- [Curating Simulated Storyworlds (James Ryan, Ch. 6F)](https://emshort.blog/2019/05/28/curating-simulated-storyworlds-james-ryan-ch-6f/) — emshort.blog, May 2019. Short's commentary on Ryan's "overgenerate and curate" method. Covers knowledge simulation (characters tracking beliefs, reports, confabulation), apophenia hacking, and the hybrid performance approach of Bad News.

## GDC Vault Talks

- [Beyond Procedural Horizons: Exploring Different Uses of Procedural Content Generation](https://gdcvault.com/play/1025536/Beyond-Procedural-Horizons-Exploring-Different) — GDC 2018 (Spirit AI). Panel featuring Short on dialogue generation challenges alongside No Man's Sky and Mini Metro authors. Documents her position at Spirit AI and her framing of procedural dialogue as a distinct generation problem from world or level generation.

- [AI Summit: Innovations in Procedural Narrative](https://gdcvault.com/play/1027846/AI-Summit-Innovations-in-Procedural) — GDC 2022 (Failbetter Games). Short, Borut Pfeifer, and Stephen Ware on advances in procedural narrative systems. Documents her transition to Failbetter and continued engagement with the AI Summit track on narrative generation.

- [Sigmoids for Storytellers: Mathematical Solutions to Narrative Problems](https://gdcvault.com/play/1027316/Sigmoids-for-Storytellers-Mathematical-Solutions) — GDC 2021 (Failbetter Games). Application of mathematical curve-shaping to narrative pacing and quality distribution problems. Connects to her quantitative approach to narrative design.

- [Choice and Character: Lessons from Writing Multiplayer Narrative Games](https://gdcvault.com/play/1016584/Choice-and-Character-Lessons-from) — GDC Online 2012 (Linden Lab). Documents her work at Linden Lab during the Versu period. Covers writing for multiplayer contexts and character-driven choice systems.

## Interactive Fiction Database

- [Galatea (IFDB)](https://ifdb.org/viewgame?id=urxrv27t7qtu52lb) — 2000. Short's landmark conversational piece treating the NPC as the dramatic subject. Cited throughout IF criticism as a foundational example of NPC-as-protagonist design.

- [Counterfeit Monkey (IFDB)](https://ifdb.org/viewgame?id=aearuuxv83plclpl) — 2012. Her most mechanically ambitious parser IF game, widely cited as a benchmark of the form.

## Professional Profile

- [Emily Short — My Work](https://emshort.blog/my-work/) — emshort.blog. Overview of her career: Galatea, Counterfeit Monkey, Blood & Laurels, Versu, Spirit AI Character Engine, Failbetter Games. Documents associate editor role at IEEE Transactions on Games and GDC AI Summit advisor role.

- [Self-Training in Narrative Design](https://emshort.blog/self-training-in-narrative-design/) — emshort.blog. Her recommended curriculum for narrative designers, covering tools (Inform 7, Twine, ChoiceScript, ink), communities (intfiction.org, IFDB), and conceptual areas (narrative structure, characterization, procedural narrative).
