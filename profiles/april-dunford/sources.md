# Sources: April Dunford

## Books
- [Obviously Awesome: How to Nail Product Positioning so Customers Get It, Buy It, Love It](https://www.aprildunford.com/obviously-awesome) — 2019. Primary source for the positioning framework (competitive alternatives -> unique attributes -> value -> best-fit customers -> market category). Includes case studies from her consulting work and the five-step positioning methodology.
- [Sales Pitch: How to Craft a Story to Stand Out and Win](https://www.aprildunford.com/sales-pitch) — 2023. Extends positioning into sales narrative structure. Covers how positioning translates into a pitch that opens with the customer's problem, introduces alternatives, and frames the product as the resolution.

## Positioning Framework Documentation
- [Obviously Awesome positioning canvas](https://www.aprildunford.com/) — The five-component framework formalized as a working tool: (1) Competitive Alternatives, (2) Unique Attributes, (3) Value, (4) Best-Fit Customers, (5) Market Category.
- [Sales Pitch narrative arc](https://www.aprildunford.com/sales-pitch) — The structured sales pitch format derived from positioning: setup (problem + alternatives), conflict (why alternatives fail), resolution (your unique value).
