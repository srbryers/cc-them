# Sources: Yu-Kai Chou

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, or posts.

## Talks & Conferences

- [Gamification to Improve Our World — TEDxLausanne](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=v5Qjuegtiyc) — TEDxLausanne. Chou's most widely viewed public talk; introduces the Octalysis Framework, the 8 Core Drives, and the human-focused design argument in 17 minutes. Reached 100,000+ viewers on YouTube.
- [TEDx: Gamification to Change the World](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-examples/tedx-gamification-change-world/) — yukaichou.com. Companion post to the TEDxLausanne talk with additional context on the framework.
- [Yu-kai Chou's Google Keynotes: A Decade of Behavioral Design Innovation](https://yukaichou.com/authority/yu-kai-chous-google-keynotes-a-decade-of-behavioral-design-innovation/) — yukaichou.com. Overview of Chou's keynote appearances at Google spanning multiple years, documenting the evolution of his framework in applied contexts.

## Books & Core Reference

- [Actionable Gamification: Beyond Points, Badges, and Leaderboards](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-book/) — yukaichou.com. The primary text; defines the Octalysis Framework, the 8 Core Drives, the Player Journey phases, White/Black Hat distinction, and the critique of pointsification. Published 2015.
- [Actionable Gamification on Google Books](https://books.google.com/books/about/Actionable_Gamification.html?id=9ZfBDwAAQBAJ) — Google Books. Freely previewable sections of the book.

## Blog Posts & Essays (yukaichou.com)

- [Octalysis Framework: Complete Gamification Guide](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-examples/octalysis-gamification-framework/) — yukaichou.com. The canonical written reference for the full Octalysis Framework: all 8 Core Drives, White/Black/Neutral Hat categorization, Left/Right Brain axis, and the octagonal diagnostic shape.
- [Human-Focused Design: The Better Term for Gamification](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-analysis/human-focused-design-better-term-gamification/) — yukaichou.com. Chou's argument for why "human-focused design" more precisely describes what gamification is — contrasted with function-focused design. Coined the term in 2012.
- [What is Gamification? A Complete Guide](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-examples/what-is-gamification/) — yukaichou.com. Definitional post; covers gamification vs. manipulation, the factory vs. theme park analogy for function-focused vs. human-focused design.
- [White Hat vs Black Hat Gamification in the Octalysis Framework](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-study/white-hat-black-hat-gamification-octalysis-framework/) — yukaichou.com. Detailed treatment of the White Hat / Black Hat distinction, which drives belong to each category, and when each is appropriate.
- [Careful Transitioning between White Hat and Black Hat Gamification](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-study/careful-transitioning-white-hat-black-hat-gamification/) — yukaichou.com. Prescriptive post on the recommended sequencing: establish White Hat first, deploy Black Hat at conversion, return to White Hat immediately after.
- [The Points, Badges, and Leaderboards Fallacy](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-analysis/pbl-fallacy/) — yukaichou.com. Chou's critique of surface-level gamification; defines pointsification and explains why PBLs without motivational architecture fail or backfire.
- [The Fallacy of Points, Badges, and Leaderboards in Gamification](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-study/points-badges-and-leaderboards-the-gamification-fallacy/) — yukaichou.com. Extended version of the PBL critique with examples of products that failed by relying on surface mechanics.
- [Black Hat Gamification: Lessons from Plants vs. Zombies 2](https://yukaichou.com/gamification-examples/black-hat-gamification-and-the-fall-of-plants-vs-zombies-2/) — yukaichou.com. Case study in the costs of Black Hat overreliance; documents how a successful franchise damaged its own engagement by shifting to loss-avoidance and scarcity mechanics.
- [The Octalysis Framework for Gamification & Behavioral Design](https://medium.com/@yukaichou/the-octalysis-framework-for-gamification-behavioral-design-fe381150f0c1) — Medium. Accessible overview of the framework for a non-specialist audience.
