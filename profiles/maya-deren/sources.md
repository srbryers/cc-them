# Sources: Maya Deren

All sources are freely available public recordings, scans, or reference pages. No paywalled sources are included. Sources marked (archive.org) can be streamed or downloaded directly.

## Primary Texts

- [An Anagram of Ideas on Art, Form and Film (1946) — full PDF](https://monoskop.org/images/3/31/Deren_Maya_An_Anagram_of_Ideas_on_Art_Form_and_Film.pdf) — Monoskop. The foundational essay published as a chapbook by The Alicat Book Shop Press, Yonkers, NY. Deren's core film theory: horizontal vs. vertical structure, ritualistic form, the poetic film, consciously applied effort vs. surrealist automatism.

- [Divine Horsemen: The Living Gods of Haiti — book scan](https://archive.org/details/divinehorsemenli00dere) — Internet Archive. Deren's 1953 ethnographic and philosophical account of Vodou. Source for: the loa as living personalities, the Rada/Petro/Gede nanchon system, possession phenomenology (the "white darkness"), syncretism as survival, community as substrate, and Deren's personal initiation and possession experience.

- [Essential Deren: Collected Writings on Film — borrowable scan](https://archive.org/details/essentialderenco0000dere) — Internet Archive. Collected essays, lectures, program notes, and manifestoes edited by Bruce R. McPherson. Includes hard-to-find articles from film journals and the complete text of An Anagram. Primary source for Deren's film theory positions.

- [Cinematography: The Creative Use of Reality — JSTOR](https://www.jstor.org/stable/20026556) — Daedalus, Vol. 89, No. 1, The Visual Arts Today (Winter 1960), pp. 150–167. Deren's final major theoretical essay, published one year before her death. Source for: the distinction between recording reality and creating it cinematically, the camera as participant, and the case for cinema as an autonomous art form.

## Films (Streaming)

- [Meshes of the Afternoon (1943) — Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/Meshes_Of_The_Afternoon_Maya_Deren) — Co-directed with Alexander Hammid. The founding work of American experimental cinema. Demonstrates Deren's techniques: spatial discontinuity, slow motion, cyclical time, the figure as formal element rather than psychological character.

- [Maya Deren Collection — Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/maya-deren-collection) — Archive collection including multiple Deren films: At Land (1944), A Study in Choreography for Camera (1945), Ritual in Transfigured Time (1946), Meditation on Violence (1948), and The Very Eye of Night (1958).

- [Divine Horsemen: The Living Gods of Haiti — documentary film (1981)](https://archive.org/details/divine-horsemen-the-living-gods-of-haiti) — Internet Archive. The posthumously assembled documentary from Deren's 18,000 feet of Haitian footage, edited by Teiji Ito and Cherel Winett Ito. Direct visual record of the Vodou ceremonies Deren documented.

## Biographical and Critical Sources

- [Maya Deren — Senses of Cinema Great Directors profile](https://www.sensesofcinema.com/2002/great-directors/deren-2/) — Senses of Cinema, Issue 23 (December 2002). Scholarly overview of Deren's biography, film theory, and legacy. Covers vertical/horizontal structure, ritualistic form, her relationship to surrealism, and her role in founding New American Cinema.

- [Maya Deren, Dance, and Gestural Encounters in Ritual in Transfigured Time — Senses of Cinema](https://www.sensesofcinema.com/2002/filmmaker-profiles/deren/) — Academic analysis of Deren's engagement with movement, gesture, and ritual across her filmography. Source for her integration of choreography and cinematic technique.

- [Maya Deren — Jewish Women's Archive Encyclopedia](https://jwa.org/encyclopedia/article/deren-maya) — Scholarly biography covering her Ukrainian Jewish origins, immigration, education at Smith College, collaboration with Hammid, Guggenheim Fellowship, Haiti fieldwork, initiation, and founding of the Creative Film Foundation.

- [Maya Deren — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_Deren) — General reference biography with citations. Source for: Guggenheim Fellowship (1946, first in motion pictures), Cannes Grand Prix International (1947), founding of the Creative Film Foundation, and overview of all major films.

## Documentary

- [In the Mirror of Maya Deren (2001) — Martina Kudlacek, director](https://zeitgeistfilms.com/film/inthemirrorofmayaderen) — Zeitgeist Films. Feature-length documentary biography. Includes interviews with contemporaries and substantial excerpts from Deren's films. Winner of the Vienna Film Prize 2001. Available for rental/streaming; excerpts and information freely accessible via the distributor page and IMDB.

## Academic Bibliography

- [Maya Deren as Film Theorist: An Annotated Bibliography — Roxanne Shirazi](https://roxanneshirazi.com/projects/maya-deren/) — Comprehensive annotated bibliography of scholarly sources on Deren as a theorist. Useful for locating additional academic treatments of her film theory and Vodou work.
