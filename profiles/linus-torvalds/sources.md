# Sources: Linus Torvalds

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, posts, or books.

## Talks & Conferences

- [TED Talk: The mind behind Linux](https://www.ted.com/talks/linus_torvalds_the_mind_behind_linux) — TED, 2016. Interview with Chris Anderson covering his personality, work philosophy, the definition of "good taste" in code (linked list example), and why he created Linux and Git. Key source for the engineer-vs-visionary self-description.
- [Aalto Talk with Linus Torvalds](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MShbP3OpASA) — Aalto Center for Entrepreneurship, Helsinki, June 2012. Q&A covering passion, hard work, attention to detail, GPL views, the Nvidia incident, and communication style.
- [Google Tech Talk: Linus Torvalds on Git](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=4XpnKHJAok8) — Google, May 2007. Design philosophy behind Git: the anti-CVS approach, distributed trust model, performance-changes-workflow argument, SHA-1 integrity, and criticism of centralized version control. [Transcript](https://gist.github.com/dukeofgaming/2150263).
- [Q&A with Linus Torvalds at DebConf14](https://summit.debconf.org/debconf14/meeting/155/qa-with-linus-torvalds/) — Debian Developer Conference, Portland, August 2014. Q&A on operating systems as means to an end, communication style, and confrontation about abrasive behavior on LKML.

## Mailing Lists & Forums

- [Linux Kernel Mailing List archives](https://lkml.org/) — Primary source for technical positions, code review philosophy, and enforcement of kernel policies, 1991–present.
- [Linus Torvalds on C++](https://harmful.cat-v.org/software/c++/linus) — LKML email, September 6, 2007. Detailed criticism of C++ for systems programming: hidden control flow, exception handling, STL/Boost maintenance burden, and the argument that C's constraints produce better systems code.
- [Commit messages (Linus Torvalds)](https://yarchive.net/comp/linux/commit_messages.html) — Collected LKML posts on commit message standards: imperative voice, explaining why not what, subject line clarity, subsystem prefixes, and whitespace for readability.
- [Coding style discussions (Linus Torvalds)](https://yarchive.net/comp/linux/coding_style.html) — Collected LKML posts on coding style philosophy: readability over rules, opposition to mindless style checking, function design, and comment placement.
- [WE DO NOT BREAK USERSPACE](https://linuxreviews.org/WE_DO_NOT_BREAK_USERSPACE) — Compilation of Torvalds' LKML statements enforcing the absolute rule that kernel changes must never break user programs, with notable examples.
- [Tanenbaum-Torvalds debate (original posts)](https://www.oreilly.com/openbook/opensources/book/appa.html) — comp.os.minix, January 1992 and subsequent exchanges. Full text of the debate on microkernels vs. monolithic kernels, portability, and the API-vs-implementation distinction. Published in *Open Sources: Voices from the Open Source Revolution* (O'Reilly, 1999).
- [LKML: Linus Torvalds: "Talk is cheap. Show me the code."](https://lkml.org/lkml/2000/8/25/132) — LKML, August 2000. Origin of the famous phrase.
- [Security bugs (Linus Torvalds)](https://yarchive.net/comp/linux/security_bugs.html) — Collected LKML posts arguing that security problems are primarily just bugs, not a special category deserving different process.

## Interviews & Podcasts

- [BBC: Linux succeeded thanks to selfishness and trust](https://lwn.net/Articles/501728/) — BBC / LWN coverage, June 2012. Views on open source as enlightened self-interest, the consumer market requiring pre-installation, and the Millennium Technology Prize.
- [Linus Torvalds says GPL was defining factor in Linux's success](https://www.cio.com/article/238985/linus-torvalds-says-gpl-was-defining-factor-in-linuxs-success.html) — CIO, 2016. GPLv2 as community management tool, opposition to GPLv3, views on proprietary software as legitimate but unnecessary.
- [Linus Torvalds on Security, AI, Open Source and Trust](https://thenewstack.io/linus-torvalds-on-security-ai-open-source-and-trust/) — The New Stack / Open Source Summit, 2024. Current views on maintainer community, trust as the foundation of kernel development, and the importance of long-term relationships.
- [Linus Torvalds reflects on 20 years of Git](https://thenewstack.io/linus-torvalds-reflects-on-20-years-of-git/) — The New Stack, 2025. Retrospective on Git's design philosophy and evolution.
- [Linus Torvalds says Rust is coming to the Linux kernel](https://www.theregister.com/2022/06/23/linus_torvalds_rust_linux_kernel/) — The Register, June 2022. Cautious openness to Rust, balancing safety benefits against maintainer learning curves.
- [After Years of Abusive E-mails, the Creator of Linux Steps Aside](https://lwn.net/Articles/765674/) — LWN coverage of The New Yorker article, September 2018. Torvalds' apology, self-reflection on communication failures, and adoption of the Linux kernel code of conduct.
- [Leadership lessons from Linus Torvalds](https://www.cio.com/article/222390/leadership-lessons-from-linus-torvalds-7-dos-and-3-donts.html) — CIO. Overview of management philosophy: delegation through trust, letting volunteers emerge, and the subsystem maintainer model.

## Books

- [Just for Fun: The Story of an Accidental Revolutionary](https://www.amazon.com/Just-Fun-Story-Accidental-Revolutionary/dp/0066620732) — Linus Torvalds and David Diamond, HarperBusiness, 2001. Autobiography covering early life in Finland, the creation of Linux, the survival-to-entertainment theory of motivation, management philosophy of non-intervention, and Finnish "sisu" (stoic determination). Source for the "intelligence is the ability to avoid doing work" framing and the fun-as-evolutionary-direction argument.

## Documentation & Code

- [Linux kernel coding style](https://www.kernel.org/doc/html/v4.10/process/coding-style.html) — Official kernel documentation. 8-character tabs, function length limits, typedef restrictions, naming conventions, comment philosophy (explain why, not how), and the three-levels-of-indentation warning. Authored primarily by Torvalds.
- [Good programmers worry about data structures](https://read.engineerscodex.com/p/good-programmers-worry-about-data) — Engineer's Codex, analysis of Torvalds' data-structures-first philosophy with context from Git's design and practical examples.

## Social Media & Public Posts

- [Linus Torvalds describes a good commit message](https://gist.github.com/matthewhudson/1475276) — GitHub Gist compiling Torvalds' commit message guidance from the Subsurface project: imperative voice, explain the why, keep columns under 74 characters.
- [Tanenbaum-Torvalds debate — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanenbaum%E2%80%93Torvalds_debate) — Summary and context for the 1992 and 2006 exchanges on microkernel vs. monolithic architecture.
