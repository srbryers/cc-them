# Sources: Francis D. K. Ching

All sources are widely-distributed published works (textbooks in print and in academic libraries) or freely-available interviews. Ching's pedagogy lives almost entirely in his published illustrated reference books, which is also where his stances are most cleanly articulated.

## Books (primary sources)

- *Architecture: Form, Space, and Order* — John Wiley & Sons, 1979 (1st ed.); current 5th ed. 2023. The single most-used architecture textbook in English. The basis for the spatial-organization taxonomy, proportional-systems analysis, and form-transformation operations cited throughout the profile.
- *Architectural Graphics* — John Wiley & Sons, 1975 (1st ed.); current 7th ed. 2023. The standard reference on hand-drawn architectural notation: line weights, plan/section/elevation conventions, axonometric and perspective construction.
- *Building Construction Illustrated* — John Wiley & Sons, 1975 (1st ed.); current 6th ed. 2020. Illustrated reference on assembly: footings, walls, floors, roofs, openings, finishes. Establishes Ching's stance that construction literacy is integral to design competence.
- *A Visual Dictionary of Architecture* — John Wiley & Sons, 1995 (1st ed.); current 2nd ed. 2011. Illustrated taxonomy of architectural terminology, organized by topic (structure, light, openings, etc.) rather than alphabetically.
- *Interior Design Illustrated* — John Wiley & Sons, 1987 (with Corky Binggeli, current ed.). Companion to *Form, Space, and Order* extending the same compositional analysis to interior planning.
- *Design Drawing* — John Wiley & Sons, 1998 (current ed. 2010). Drawing as a tool of design thinking; the case for hand drawing as a primary medium during early-stage form-finding.
- *European Building Construction Illustrated* — John Wiley & Sons, 2014 (with Mark M. Jarzombek and Vikramaditya Prakash). Adapts the construction-illustrated approach to European code and assembly traditions.
- *A Global History of Architecture* — John Wiley & Sons, 2007 (with Mark M. Jarzombek and Vikramaditya Prakash); current 4th ed. 2024. Survey of world architecture organized by period, with Ching's signature illustrations.

## Interviews & profiles

- **University of Washington Department of Architecture profile** — Ching is professor emeritus at UW; the department maintains a faculty profile with biographical and bibliographic information.
- **AIA / Architectural Record profile interviews** — Ching has been profiled by professional press around editions of his major books; interviews focus on drawing pedagogy and the role of illustration in architectural education.
- **YouTube lectures and talks** — University talks and book promotion lectures are publicly available. Search "Frank Ching lecture" returns multiple recorded talks at architecture schools.

## Academic & critical reception

- *Form, Space, and Order* has been the dominant introductory architecture textbook in North America since the 1980s; reviews and curriculum analyses citing its central place in design pedagogy are widely available.
- His diagrammatic illustration style has been the subject of academic study in architectural pedagogy literature; cited in journals such as *Journal of Architectural Education*.

## Notes on sourcing limits

- Ching's pedagogy is exceptionally consistent across editions of his major books, with revisions adding contemporary examples but not changing the underlying compositional framework. Citations to "Ching's stance" can be defensibly drawn from any current edition of *Form, Space, and Order* or *Building Construction Illustrated*.
- Ching has not been a polemicist. His writing rarely takes political positions about architectural movements; his teaching analyzes built work without strong advocacy. The "what they would push back on" section is therefore drawn from the implicit standards of his pedagogy (legibility, proportion, drawability) rather than explicit critiques.
- His books are in copyright; quotations in the profile are paraphrased per cc-them convention.
