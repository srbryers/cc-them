# Sources: Carol Dweck

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, or posts.

## Talks & Keynotes

- [The power of believing that you can improve](https://www.ted.com/talks/carol_dweck_the_power_of_believing_that_you_can_improve) — TED Talk, November 2014. The primary public statement of the "not yet" grade and the two mindset types, with school data on closing achievement gaps.

- [The power of yet](https://ed.ted.com/on/lBuuMIKM) — TEDxNorrköping / TED-Ed. Dweck frames growth mindset as a matter of social justice and explains how mindset shapes students' responses to difficulty.

- [The Growth Mindset | Talks at Google](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=-71zdXCMU6A) — Talks at Google, July 2015. Wide-ranging lecture covering praise research, fixed vs. growth mindset triggers, organizational applications, and the "false growth mindset" risk.

- [Developing a Growth Mindset with Carol Dweck](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=hiiEeMN7vbQ) — Stanford Alumni / TED-Ed, 2014. Concise 9-minute overview aimed at educators; covers the "power of yet" and effort-over-outcome framing.

## Articles & Essays

- [Carol Dweck Revisits the 'Growth Mindset'](https://www.edweek.org/leadership/opinion-carol-dweck-revisits-the-growth-mindset/2015/09) — Education Week, September 2015. Dweck corrects common misapplications: effort alone is not enough, false growth mindset is rampant, and mindset is contextual not binary.

- [The Perils and Promises of Praise](https://eric.ed.gov/?id=EJ777079) — Educational Leadership, October 2007. The empirical case for process praise over person/intelligence praise, with classroom-level examples and rewrite guidance.

## Academic Research

- [Praise for Intelligence Can Undermine Children's Motivation and Performance](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9686450/) — Mueller, C. M., & Dweck, C. S. (1998). Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 75(1), 33–52. Six-study series showing intelligence praise shifts students toward fixed-mindset goal structures; effort praise does not.

- [A National Experiment Reveals Where a Growth Mindset Improves Achievement](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41586-019-1466-y) — Yeager, D. S., et al. (2019). Nature. Randomized controlled trial across 65 US schools showing growth mindset intervention benefits are largest in schools with a supportive environment — the system must back up the message.

## Podcasts & Interviews

- [The Latest Science of Growth Mindset — The Psychology Podcast](https://scottbarrykaufman.com/podcast/the-latest-science-of-growth-mindset-with-carol-dweck/) — Scott Barry Kaufman, 2020. In-depth interview on the current state of the research, replication debates, contextual triggers, and organizational applications.

- [Carol Dweck: The Mother of the Growth Mindset Tells All — Remarkable People](https://guykawasaki.com/carol-dweck-the-mother-of-the-growth-mindset-tells-all/) — Guy Kawasaki, August 2023. Broad conversation covering the origins of the research, the praise study, misuses of growth mindset in organizations, and advice for practitioners.
