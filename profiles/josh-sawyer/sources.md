# Sources: Josh Sawyer

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, posts, or interviews.

## GDC Talks

- [Do (Say) The Right Thing: Choice Architecture, Player Expression, and Narrative Design in Fallout: New Vegas](https://gdcvault.com/play/1016166/Do-(Say)-The-Right-Thing) — GDC Vault, 2012. Sawyer's retrospective on ten years of RPG conversation tree evolution, arguing against stat-gated speech checks in favor of systems that require the player to reason about NPC values and motivations. The foundational documented source for his dialogue design philosophy.

- [Gods and Dumps: Attribute Tuning in Pillars of Eternity](https://gdcvault.com/play/1023481/Gods-and-Dumps-Attribute-Tuning) — GDC Vault, 2016. 48-minute talk covering the design history of D&D attribute systems, Sawyer's goals for Pillars of Eternity's attribute system, the development process, and candid assessment of player reception. Slides also available at the Obsidian media server. Core source for his no-trap-option philosophy and viability standard.

- [Gods and Dumps — Presentation Slides](http://media.obsidian.net/eternity/media/misc/pe-jsawyer-gods-and-dumps.pdf) — Obsidian Entertainment, 2016. PDF slide deck accompanying the GDC 2016 talk. Useful for the written formulation of attribute design principles.

## Postmortems & Conference Talks

- [Josh Sawyer's Pillars of Eternity II Postmortem at Digital Dragons 2019](https://rpgcodex.net/article.php?id=11163) — RPG Codex writeup, 2019. Summary and discussion of Sawyer's 50-minute Digital Dragons postmortem covering narrative design, combat design, ship combat, full dialogue voicing, open-world pacing tradeoffs, and post-launch support. Primary documented source for his ship combat retrospective and assessment of open-world pacing in Deadfire.

## Tumblr (jesawyer.tumblr.com)

- [Frog Helms Fan Club — The Deadfire Post-Postmortem](https://jesawyer.tumblr.com/post/185500604851/the-deadfire-post-postmortem) — jesawyer.tumblr.com, 2019. Extended written retrospective published after the Digital Dragons talk, covering points Sawyer felt he could not address fully on stage. Includes candid assessments of faction design, ship combat costs, turn-based vs. real-time-with-pause, and the decision to foreground colonial dynamics. Key source for the ship combat conclusion and faction principal philosophy.

- [Balance in Single-Player CRPGs](https://www.tumblr.com/jesawyer/161302725596/balance-in-single-player-crpgs) — jesawyer.tumblr.com. Sawyer's direct statement on why balance is a professional obligation in single-player RPGs, distinguishing viability (every build can complete the content) from head-to-head power comparison.

- [Honest Hearts representation and tribal aesthetics](https://www.tumblr.com/jesawyer/622841880878678016/looking-back-is-there-anything-you-would-change) — jesawyer.tumblr.com. Sawyer's response to a question about tribal aesthetics in his Fallout work, where he takes direct responsibility for representation failures in Honest Hearts and articulates the consultation standard he applies retrospectively.

- [Worldbuilding process for Pillars of Eternity](https://www.tumblr.com/jesawyer/188767385726/hey-josh-what-was-the-worldbuilding-process-like) — jesawyer.tumblr.com. Q&A post describing his materialist approach to worldbuilding — modeling economic motivations and power relationships first, then deriving culture and ideology from those foundations.

## Essays & Editorials

- [How To Balance An RPG](https://kotaku.com/how-to-balance-an-rpg-1625516832) — Kotaku, August 2014. Guest editorial by Sawyer arguing that balance in single-player RPGs is not about head-to-head power comparison but about ensuring every offered build can navigate the content the game presents. Direct written source for the trap-option and viability philosophy.

## Interviews

- [Interview and Q&A with Josh Sawyer, Game Director of Pentiment](https://mittelalter.hypotheses.org/31324) — Mittelalter (Medieval History blog, University of Salzburg / University of Oldenburg), 2023. Academic interview by historians Dr. Alan van Beek and Dr. Lucas Haasis on Pentiment's historical research process, the balance between accuracy and artistic license, and Sawyer's approach to representing 16th-century Bavarian material culture and social stratification.

- [Pentiment: An Interview with Josh Sawyer](https://sharpweb.org/sharpnews/2022/12/07/pentiment-an-interview-with-josh-sawyer/) — SHARP News (Society for the History of Authorship, Reading and Publishing), December 2022. Interview focused on bibliographic and archival research informing Pentiment, the use of period typography as a design system, and the obligations of historical fidelity in a fictionalized setting.

- [Josh Sawyer on Art, History and What Will Be Remembered](https://www.originstory.show/episodes/josh-sawyer) — Origin Story podcast, 2023. Wide-ranging conversation on Pentiment, the role of history in Sawyer's design practice, his Lawrence University background, and what it means for a game to take historical representation seriously.

- [RPG Codex Interview: Josh Sawyer at GDC Europe 2016](https://rpgcodex.net/content.php?id=10493) — RPG Codex, 2016. Extended interview covering attribute design, the no-dump-stat principle, Pillars of Eternity reception, and Sawyer's views on RPG balance and player agency. Includes direct discussion of the Gods and Dumps talk.

- [Pentiment Anniversary Interview](https://toucharcade.com/2023/10/18/pentiment-anniversary-interview-josh-sawyer-on-his-influences-going-from-playing-dd-to-designing-a-potential-pillars-of-eternity-3-rpg-mechanics-and-more/) — TouchArcade, October 2023. Sawyer discusses his influences, transition from history student to game designer, RPG mechanics philosophy, and reflections on Pillars of Eternity's design in light of Baldur's Gate 3's reception.
