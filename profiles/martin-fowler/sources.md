# Sources: Martin Fowler

All sources are freely available public recordings, blog posts, or articles.

## Books (Summary / Free Excerpts)

- [Refactoring: Improving the Design of Existing Code — Book Page](https://martinfowler.com/books/refactoring.html) — martinfowler.com. Overview and rationale for the second edition. The canonical reference for Fowler's refactoring vocabulary and two-hats discipline.
- [Patterns of Enterprise Application Architecture — Book Page](https://martinfowler.com/books/eaa.html) — martinfowler.com. Catalog of enterprise patterns including Repository, Gateway, Service Layer, and Unit of Work.

## Talks & Conference Videos

- [The Many Meanings of Event-Driven Architecture — GOTO Chicago 2017](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=STKCRSUsyP0) — YouTube / GOTO Conferences. 50-minute keynote distinguishing event notification, event-carried state transfer, event sourcing, and CQRS. One of Fowler's most-cited talks.
- [Event Sourcing — YOW! 2016](https://www.classcentral.com/course/youtube-event-sourcing-martin-fowler-yow-2016-193517) — YouTube via Class Central. 28-minute talk describing event sourcing, its advantages, and trade-offs. First widely-distributed video treatment of this talk.
- [When to Use Microservices (And When Not To!) — with Sam Newman, GOTO 2020](https://www.classcentral.com/course/youtube-when-to-use-microservices-and-when-not-to-sam-newman-martin-fowler-275242) — YouTube via Class Central. Practical conversation on the prerequisites and costs of a microservices architecture.
- [Videos of Talks — martinfowler.com](https://martinfowler.com/videos.html) — Full index of recorded conference presentations maintained by Fowler himself.

## Articles & Bliki Posts (martinfowler.com)

- [Microservices — defining the architectural style](https://martinfowler.com/articles/microservices.html) — martinfowler.com, 2014. Co-authored with James Lewis. The article that popularised the term "microservices" and catalogued the characteristic properties of that style.
- [Microservice Trade-Offs](https://martinfowler.com/articles/microservice-trade-offs.html) — martinfowler.com. Honest accounting of the costs microservices impose: distribution, eventual consistency, operational complexity.
- [Microservice Prerequisites](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/MicroservicePrerequisites.html) — martinfowler.com bliki. Rapid provisioning, basic monitoring, and deployment automation as non-negotiable prerequisites before adopting microservices.
- [Is High Quality Software Worth the Cost?](https://martinfowler.com/articles/is-quality-worth-cost.html) — martinfowler.com, 2019. Argues that the cost of high internal quality is effectively negative: neglecting quality only pays off for very short delivery horizons.
- [Design Stamina Hypothesis](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/DesignStaminaHypothesis.html) — martinfowler.com bliki. The hypothesis that investing in design yields compounding velocity gains that overtake the apparent short-term speed of neglecting design.
- [CQRS](https://martinfowler.com/bliki/CQRS.html) — martinfowler.com bliki. Definition of Command Query Responsibility Segregation as introduced by Greg Young; Fowler's assessment of when it is and is not appropriate.
- [What do you mean by "Event-Driven"?](https://martinfowler.com/articles/201701-event-driven.html) — martinfowler.com, 2017. Written companion to the GOTO 2017 keynote; taxonomises the four event-driven patterns.
- [Technical Debt tag](https://martinfowler.com/tags/technical%20debt.html) — martinfowler.com. Index of all bliki entries and articles on technical debt, including the Technical Debt Quadrant.
- [Domain Driven Design tag](https://martinfowler.com/tags/domain%20driven%20design.html) — martinfowler.com. Index of Fowler's writing on DDD, bounded contexts, ubiquitous language, and related patterns.

## Articles on Agile & XP

- [Is Design Dead?](https://martinfowler.com/articles/designDead.html) — martinfowler.com. Long essay from 2000/2004 arguing that XP and evolutionary design do not eliminate the need for software design — they demand a deeper understanding of it.
