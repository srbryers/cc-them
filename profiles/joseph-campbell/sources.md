# Sources: Joseph Campbell

All sources are freely available public recordings, transcripts, or web resources. No paywalled sources.

## Primary Video — The Power of Myth (PBS, 1988)

- [Joseph Campbell and The Power of Myth — BillMoyers.com series page](https://billmoyers.com/series/joseph-campbell-and-the-power-of-myth-1988/) — Official archive at BillMoyers.com. Six episodes: "The Hero's Adventure," "The Message of the Myth," "The First Storytellers," "Sacrifice and Bliss," "Love and the Goddess," "Masks of Eternity." Filmed at Skywalker Ranch and the American Museum of Natural History, 1985–1986. Broadcast posthumously on PBS in 1988. Primary source for Campbell's views on the monomyth, follow your bliss, ritual, the four functions, and mythology in modernity.

- [Ep. 1: The Hero's Adventure — audio transcript, BillMoyers.com](https://billmoyers.com/content/ep-1-joseph-campbell-and-the-power-of-myth-the-hero%E2%80%99s-adventure-audio/) — Audio and partial transcript of Episode 1. Covers the call to adventure, the hero's journey stages, and Campbell's views on Star Wars as modern mythology.

- [The Power of Myth — Complete PBS Series, Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/power_of_myth) — Full six-part series available for free streaming and download. MKV format. The most accessible free version of the complete series.

## Primary Video — Mythos Documentary Series

- [Joseph Campbell's Mythos Documentary Series on YouTube — LearnOutLoud.com announcement](https://www.learnoutloud.com/content/blog/archives/2026/03/watch-joseph-campbells-mythos-documentary-series-on-youtube.php) — Documents the Joseph Campbell Foundation's upload of Mythos Volumes I and II to their YouTube channel. Each volume is approximately 5 hours of lectures recorded over the last six years of Campbell's life. Susan Sarandon narrates. Covers comparative mythology, psychology, Native American mythology, Hinduism, and Buddhism.

## Joseph Campbell Foundation (jcf.org) — Educational Resources

- [About Joseph Campbell — Biography, JCF](https://www.jcf.org/learn/joseph-campbell-biography) — Official biography from the Joseph Campbell Foundation. Documents his academic career at Sarah Lawrence College (1934–1972), his intellectual formation, key works, and the founding of JCF in 1991.

- [Joseph Campbell and the Hero's Journey — JCF educational resource](https://www.jcf.org/learn/joseph-campbell-heros-journey) — JCF's primary educational page on the monomyth. Covers the three-stage structure (separation, initiation, return), the 17 substages, and the psychological interpretation of the hero's journey as a model for living.

- [The Collected Works of Joseph Campbell — JCF](https://www.jcf.org/learn/joseph-campbell-collected-works) — Overview of the full Collected Works series published by New World Library / JCF. Includes descriptions of all major works: *The Hero with a Thousand Faces*, *The Masks of God* tetralogy, *Myths to Live By*, *The Inner Reaches of Outer Space*, *Pathways to Bliss*, and others.

- [The Collected Audio of Joseph Campbell — JCF](https://www.jcf.org/learn/collected-audio-of-joseph-campbell) — JCF's archive of restored audio recordings of Campbell's lectures from the 1970s and 1980s, originally recorded at Esalen Institute, the Jung Institute of San Francisco, UCSF Extension, and other venues. The foundational source for his spoken philosophy.

## Open Culture — Lecture Archives

- [48 Hours of Joseph Campbell Lectures Free Online — Open Culture (2015)](https://www.openculture.com/2015/08/48-hours-of-joseph-campbell-lectures-free-online.html) — Open Culture's compilation of freely available Campbell lectures. Covers the Power of Myth series and additional lecture recordings. Useful index of publicly accessible primary material.

- [Hear 48 Hours of Lectures by Joseph Campbell — Open Culture (2018)](https://www.openculture.com/2018/03/hear-48-hours-of-lectures-by-joseph-campbell-on-comparative-mythology-and-the-heros-journey.html) — Updated Open Culture post linking to lecture recordings on Spotify and other free platforms. Documents what is publicly accessible across comparative mythology and the hero's journey topics.

## Wikipedia — Reference Summaries

- [The Hero with a Thousand Faces — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hero_with_a_Thousand_Faces) — Summary of the 1949 book, the monomyth concept, the 17-stage structure, and the book's influence on George Lucas and subsequent popular culture.

- [Joseph Campbell — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Campbell) — Biographical overview covering his academic career, intellectual influences (Joyce, Jung, van Gennep, Zimmer), major works, and the reception and criticism of his universalist approach (including critiques by Wendy Doniger and others).

- [Joseph Campbell and the Power of Myth — Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Campbell_and_the_Power_of_Myth) — Production history of the 1988 PBS series, episode descriptions, filming locations, and the series' impact on public understanding of mythology.

## Academic / Archive

- [Myths to Live By — Internet Archive (free)](https://archive.org/details/mythstoliveby00camp_0) — Digitized copy of the 1972 collection of Cooper Union Forum lectures (1958–1971). Primary source for Campbell's views on myth and modernity, East/West contrasts, the mythology of love, and schizophrenia as the inward journey.

- [Joseph Campbell, A Scholar's Life — folkstory.com](https://folkstory.com/campbell/scholars_life.html) — Detailed biographical essay covering Campbell's formative years, his period of self-directed reading during the Great Depression (1929–1934), his teaching at Sarah Lawrence, and the intellectual relationships that shaped his method.
